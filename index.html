Current Research

Thermodynamic Efficiency of Nuclear Thermal Rockets (NTR)

My current research interests focus on increasing thermodynamic efficiency for nuclear thermal propulsion (NTP) methods for a Mars based mission. Traditionally, nuclear reactor cooling channels have a circular geometry. However, with current additive manufacturing technologies, complex geometries are able to be manufactured and studied for future use. If other non-circular cross sectional cooling channels are able to increase the thermodynamic efficiency of the reactor, said geometry may be worth pursuing for further study.

Controllable Hovering Cold Gas Thruster (CGT)

In addition to the NTR research, I also serve as the graduate advisor and program manager (PM) for the ARA controllable CGT project. As the PM of the CGT team, I oversee the budget and timeline for the research, design, analysis, and development of an omnidirectional controllable inert gas thruster system.
